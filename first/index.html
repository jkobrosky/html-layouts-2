With a solid bug-fixing system in place we're in a much better position to do great work, yet it's important to note that good bug-reporting skills are not only a requirement for developers. Everyone on the team should be capable of finding a bug and providing as much information as possible about it. This means that designers, developers, and project managers of all experience levels shouldn't be afraid to use the bug tool and get an overview of the project as a whole. I've always found that Trello has been my personal favourite, as it makes a lot more sense to the rest of the team, but that doesn't necessarily mean that it's the best for your particular use-case.
